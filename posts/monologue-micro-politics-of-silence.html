Monologue

Micro-politics of Silence

with Maya Ortiz • Recorded May 2025

Razdaan: Silence is often framed as the absence of speech, yet in the seminars we run together, silence feels deliberate. Intentional. What are the politics at play?

Maya Ortiz: Silence is a refusal to perform on demand. In activist circles, we are asked to testify endlessly. Choosing silence can protect the body from extraction. It is a care practice.

Razdaan: We see this when participants place a stone in the center of the circle—a sign that the next minute belongs to collective quiet. The room shifts: people start noticing breath, posture, the hum of traffic outside.

Maya Ortiz: That shift is political. It disrupts the tempo of productivity. It reminds us that presence does not require constant disclosure. Silence becomes a commons we tend together.
