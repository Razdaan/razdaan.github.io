After Authenticity

18 minute read • Published September 7, 2025

Authenticity once promised relief from performance. In late capitalism, the promise feels inverted: sincerity is monetized, vulnerability becomes a brand pillar, and the search for the "real" self folds into a growth metric. After authenticity comes atmospheric identity, built less on essence and more on choreography.

The Atmospheric Self

The atmospheric self is diffuse, ambient, ever-adjusting. Drawing on Baudrillard's simulacra, we can read the atmospheric self as the fourth order—an arrangement of references without origin. The question shifts from "Who am I?" to "What texture am I emitting?" Influencers answer by curating moods. Institutions respond by commodifying intimacy.

Rather than dismiss this as mere artifice, we might explore how the atmospheric self can become a site of resistance. To curate a mood is to design a space. What if we designed for slowness, opacity, refusal?

Practices of Opacity

Édouard Glissant defends opacity as a right: the right not to be fully legible to the dominant gaze. In the atmosphere economy, opacity is a tactic. A philosopher's blog can enact opacity by holding back certain references, by foregrounding questions over declarations.

This site experiments with opacity through layered navigation. The manifesto is visible, yet the annotations remain invitations to those who linger. Authenticity yields to hospitality—an offering rather than revelation.

Ethical Choreographies

After authenticity, ethics look like choreography: ongoing adjustments that keep bodies in relation without demanding sameness. We rehearse ethics through salons, walking seminars, and co-authored bibliographies. The work is imperfect, iterative, yet alive.
