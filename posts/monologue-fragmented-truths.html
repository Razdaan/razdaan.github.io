Monologue

On Fragmented Truths

with Dr. Saira Ney • Recorded July 2025

Razdaan: We often hear that postmodernism dethrones truth. Yet in our work with community organizers, this "dethroning" feels more like an invitation to hold multiple truths at once. How do we keep plurality from dissolving into indifference?

Dr. Saira Ney: By returning to material consequence. Multiple truths do not free us from accountability. They multiply the sites where care must be practiced. When the city hears conflicting testimonies, the response should not be paralysis but a deeper listening infrastructure.

Razdaan: That resonates with the research salons we've been hosting. Each session begins with a "truth fragment"—a participant's situated claim. Rather than debate accuracy, we map the conditions that produced it. The mapping often reveals neglected responsibility.

Dr. Saira Ney: Exactly. Postmodernism's critique of grand narratives is frequently misread as a dismissal of commitment. I see it as a call to recommit, but with finer grain. Truth fragments demand local stewardship.
